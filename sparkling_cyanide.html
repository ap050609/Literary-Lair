Agatha Christie — Character Dossiers

Sparkling Cyanide

Six Suspects. One Deadly Evening. No Survivors of Innocence.

The Victim
Rosemary Barton Beautiful. Brilliant. Buried.
Background
A beautiful, wealthy, and socially admired woman of high society — known for her charm, glamour, and the secrets she kept close.
Personality
Magnetic, confident, and enigmatic. She enjoyed attention and luxury while concealing the darker corners of her life.
Key Traits
Charismatic Impulsive Emotionally Complex
Motive Against Her
Her wealth, romantic entanglements, and hidden secrets bred jealousy, resentment, and financial temptation among those closest to her.
R
The Husband
George Barton Devoted. Determined. Dangerous?
Background
A successful businessman and Rosemary's husband. After her death, he becomes consumed by one singular purpose: to uncover the truth.
Personality
Reserved, intelligent, and emotionally restrained. Initially appears devoted, though deeper tensions within the marriage may tell another story.
Key Traits
Analytical Controlled Determined
Suspected Motive
Financial gain through inheritance and a simmering jealousy over Rosemary's suspected romantic affairs outside their marriage.
G
The Sister
Iris Marle Overlooked. Observant. Free?
Background
Rosemary's quieter, less flamboyant younger sister — a woman who spent her life in the long shadow cast by her glamorous sibling.
Personality
Gentle, thoughtful, and somewhat insecure. She struggles beneath the weight of constant comparison to the woman the world adored.
Key Traits
Sensitive Modest Observant
Suspected Motive
Rosemary's death could bring financial inheritance — and, at last, liberation from a life spent diminished beside a brighter flame.
I
The Lover
Anthony Browne Passionate. Possessive. Unravelling.
Background
A passionate and somewhat reckless man, deeply entangled in a romantic affair with Rosemary — an affair that consumed him entirely.
Personality
Intense, emotional, and impulsive. His devotion walked the fine and terrifying line between love and obsession.
Key Traits
Passionate Dramatic Possessive
Suspected Motive
Romantic jealousy, obsessive attachment, and the emotional turmoil of a man who could not bear to share — or lose — what he believed was his.
A
The Secretary
Ruth Lessing Efficient. Ambitious. Calculating.
Background
Efficient and highly intelligent, she works in close proximity to George Barton — quietly managing his affairs with immaculate discretion.
Personality
Ambitious, controlled, and keenly observant. Behind her professional composure lies a woman of formidable personal ambition and quiet longing.
Key Traits
Calculating Disciplined Discreet
Suspected Motive
A secret love for George and the tantalising prospect of ascending — socially and financially — if Rosemary were no longer in the picture.
L
The Investigator
Colonel Race Perceptive. Patient. Relentless.
Background
A seasoned investigator with a distinguished record in complex, high-stakes cases. He has seen the worst of human nature — and remained unmoved.
Personality
Calm, logical, and highly perceptive. He reads people the way others read books — carefully, quietly, and with devastating accuracy.
Key Traits
Analytical Patient Authoritative
Personal Motive
None. He serves only one master: the truth — and he will not rest until it is laid bare for all to see.
